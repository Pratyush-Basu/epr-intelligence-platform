EXTENDED PRODUCER RESPONSIBILITY (EPR) COMPLIANCE GUIDE
For Plastic Waste Management — India
Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) Guidelines

=======================================================================
SECTION 1: INTRODUCTION TO EPR
=======================================================================

Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) is a policy approach under which
producers are given significant responsibility (physical or financial)
for the treatment or disposal of post-consumer products.

In India, the Plastic Waste Management Rules 2016 (amended in 2022) mandate
that all producers, importers, and brand owners (PIBOs) of plastic packaging
must fulfill EPR targets. The rules are enforced by the Central Pollution
Control Board (CPCB) and State Pollution Control Boards (SPCBs).

EPR obligations apply to:
- Producers who manufacture plastic packaging materials
- Importers who bring plastic-packaged goods into India
- Brand owners who use plastic packaging for their products

=======================================================================
SECTION 2: CATEGORIES OF PLASTIC PACKAGING
=======================================================================

Under EPR rules, plastic packaging is classified into four categories:

Category I — Rigid Plastic Packaging:
- Includes plastic bottles, containers, jars, boxes, and crates
- Must be collected and recycled or sent to co-processing facilities
- Minimum recycling target: 50% of plastic introduced in the market

Category II — Flexible Plastic Packaging:
- Includes pouches, sachets, wrappers, carry bags, and films
- More challenging to collect and recycle due to contamination
- Must meet minimum collection targets set by CPCB
- Often subject to co-processing as end-of-life option

Category III — Multilayer Plastic Packaging:
- Composed of alternating layers of plastic with other materials
- Includes laminates, tetra packs, and composite packaging
- Requires specialized recycling infrastructure
- EPR targets increasing annually towards full accountability

Category IV — Extended Plastic Packaging (Compostable):
- Includes certified compostable plastic under IS 17088:2008
- Exempt from standard EPR targets if certified
- Producers must obtain certification from accredited labs

=======================================================================
SECTION 3: REGISTRATION AND COMPLIANCE PROCESS
=======================================================================

Step 1 — Registration on EPR Portal:
All PIBOs must register on the centralized EPR portal maintained by CPCB
at eprs.cpcb.gov.in. Registration requires submitting company details,
production volumes, and plastic categories.

Step 2 — Annual Target Setting:
Based on plastic introduced into the market (PIM), CPCB assigns annual
EPR targets. Targets are expressed as percentages of PIM per category.

Step 3 — Monthly Declaration:
PIBOs must submit monthly declarations of:
(a) Plastic quantity produced or imported
(b) Packaging category breakdown
(c) Evidence of collection and processing

Step 4 — ERP Reconciliation:
Declared quantities are reconciled against ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning)
procurement and production data. Any discrepancy above 5% triggers a
compliance review.

Step 5 — Certificate Procurement:
Producers must procure EPR certificates from registered recyclers or
waste processors. Certificates are issued per metric ton of plastic processed.

Step 6 — Annual Filing:
By June 30 each year, all PIBOs must file annual returns including:
- Total plastic introduced in market
- Total EPR certificates procured
- Surplus or deficit position
- Plans for next year

=======================================================================
SECTION 4: EPR TARGETS AND TIMELINES
=======================================================================

Financial Year 2024-25:
- Rigid Plastic: 50% collection and recycling
- Flexible Plastic: 30% collection and processing
- Multilayer Plastic: 25% collection and processing

Financial Year 2025-26:
- Rigid Plastic: 60% collection and recycling
- Flexible Plastic: 45% collection and processing
- Multilayer Plastic: 40% collection and processing

Financial Year 2026-27 (proposed):
- Rigid Plastic: 70% collection and recycling
- Flexible Plastic: 60% collection and processing
- Multilayer Plastic: 55% collection and processing

Extended Producers that fail to meet annual targets are liable to:
- Environmental Compensation as prescribed by NGT
- Suspension of EPR registration
- Prohibition on introduction of new plastic in market

=======================================================================
SECTION 5: PLASTIC INTRODUCED IN MARKET (PIM) CALCULATION
=======================================================================

PIM is calculated as:

PIM = Raw Material Procured + Opening Stock − Closing Stock − Rejected/Waste

For plastic packaging manufacturers:
PIM = Packaging Produced and Dispatched for Sale

Producers must maintain monthly records of:
- Raw material procurement (kg by category)
- Production outputs (kg by category)
- Inventory movements
- Dispatches to customers

All records must be reconciled with financial accounts and GST invoices.
Discrepancies between declared PIM and ERP-computed PIM above 5% require
formal explanation to CPCB within 30 days.

=======================================================================
SECTION 6: EPR CERTIFICATES
=======================================================================

EPR Certificates are digital credits issued by CPCB-registered waste
processors and recyclers when they process plastic waste.

Types of EPR Certificates:
1. Recycling Certificate — issued for plastic recycled into secondary material
2. Co-processing Certificate — issued for plastic used as fuel in cement kilns
3. Industrial Composting Certificate — for certified compostable plastics

Certificate Validity:
- EPR Certificates are valid for one financial year
- Unused certificates can be banked for up to two years
- Certificates must be linked to producer's EPR account on portal

Procurement of Certificates:
Producers can procure certificates from:
- Registered plastic waste processors (direct procurement)
- EPR marketplace on CPCB portal (trading platform)
- Organized collection agencies with processing tie-ups

=======================================================================
SECTION 7: PENALTIES AND ENFORCEMENT
=======================================================================

Non-compliance with EPR obligations may result in:

1. Environmental Compensation (EC):
   - Calculated based on shortfall in EPR targets
   - Rate: INR 10,000 per metric ton of shortfall for rigid plastic
   - Rate: INR 15,000 per metric ton for flexible and multilayer
   
2. Registration Suspension:
   - CPCB may suspend EPR registration for repeated non-compliance
   - Business operations may be halted until compliance is restored

3. Legal Action:
   - Under Environment Protection Act 1986
   - Criminal liability for willful misreporting

4. Public Disclosure:
   - Names of defaulting producers published on CPCB website
   - Reputational risk for brand owners

=======================================================================
SECTION 8: EPR OBLIGATIONS FOR PLASTIC PRODUCERS
=======================================================================

All plastic producers must:

1. Register with CPCB EPR portal before commencing production
2. Maintain category-wise plastic quantity records monthly
3. Submit monthly declarations within 15 days of month end
4. Procure EPR certificates equivalent to EPR targets
5. Submit annual EPR return by June 30
6. Retain records for minimum 7 years for audit purposes
7. Allow CPCB/SPCB officials to inspect production facilities
8. Report changes in production capacity within 30 days
9. Notify CPCB of any facility closure or change in business

For rigid plastic producers specifically:
- Must establish or participate in Producer Responsibility Organization (PRO)
- PRO coordinates collection from consumers and commercial establishments
- Monthly reconciliation of collection data with EPR targets is mandatory

=======================================================================
SECTION 9: PRODUCER RESPONSIBILITY ORGANIZATIONS (PROs)
=======================================================================

A PRO is a third-party organization authorized by CPCB to assist producers
in meeting their EPR obligations.

PRO Responsibilities:
- Collection of post-consumer plastic waste from designated areas
- Channeling waste to registered recyclers and processors
- Issuing EPR certificates on behalf of producers
- Monthly reporting to CPCB on collection and processing volumes

Joining a PRO:
- Producers may join existing CPCB-registered PROs
- PRO membership fees vary by company size and plastic volume
- PROs typically offer bundled EPR certificate procurement

SECTION 10: DIGITAL COMPLIANCE TOOLS
=======================================================================

Modern compliance management requires robust digital infrastructure:

1. ERP Integration:
   - EPR compliance data must be reconciled with ERP systems
   - Monthly plastic quantity data extracted from production modules
   - Variance reports generated for discrepancies above 5%

2. AI-Assisted Compliance:
   - Natural Language Processing for compliance Q&A
   - Automated anomaly detection in production data
   - Predictive analytics for EPR target forecasting

3. Document Management:
   - Digital storage of EPR certificates
   - Automated expiry alerts for certificates
   - Audit trail maintenance for all submissions

4. Reporting Dashboards:
   - Real-time visibility into EPR position (surplus/deficit)
   - Category-wise plastic tracking
   - Trend analysis across months and years

=======================================================================
SECTION 11: COMMON COMPLIANCE MISTAKES
=======================================================================

1. Incorrect Category Classification:
   - Multilayer packaging often miscategorized as flexible
   - Results in incorrect target calculation

2. Delayed Monthly Declarations:
   - Late submissions attract penalty notices from CPCB
   - Submit within 15 days of month end

3. ERP Data Mismatch:
   - Declared quantities inconsistent with ERP procurement data
   - Common cause: rounding errors in unit conversion (grams to kg)
   - Solution: standardize on kg across all systems

4. Certificate Shortfall:
   - Not procuring sufficient EPR certificates before year end
   - Plan certificate procurement quarterly, not annually

5. Unreported Inventory:
   - Closing stock adjustments not reflected in declarations
   - Maintain monthly inventory reconciliation as SOP
